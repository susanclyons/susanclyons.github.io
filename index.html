Susan Lyons, PhD

I am an independent consultant interested in the intersection of educational measurement and social justice. I work with clients to leverage the power of assessment to help create a more equitable future.

In addition to my consulting work, I am the Executive Director of a non-profit organization aimed at advancing gender and racial equity in the field of educational measurement, Women in Measurement, Inc.

Consulting Services

Design

I partner with clients to design innovative solutions related to educational assessment and accountability. I engage with my clients and their stakeholders to understand their goals, develop a strategy, and deliver creative solutions to complex problems.

Teach

I develop and deliver sustained professional development opportunities for teachers, leaders, and assessment professionals. Topics include assessment for learning, culturally-sustaining assessment, & re-imagining school accountability.

Write

I collaborate with clients and colleagues to develop written products and presentations that advance our thinking and share our work. My portfolio includes academic research, technical reports, policy briefs, toolkits, and blog posts. 

Advise

I serve as a technical adviser to assessment and accountability programs. I have particular interest and expertise in offering technical solutions under policy constraints in order to advance innovation in educational assessment.

I am working closely with the Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion team at Cognia on a number of special projects aimed at advancing racial equity in educational measurement. As one of these projects, I am leading the writing of an equitable assessment toolkit in which we identify five areas of opportunity for interrupting patterns of privilege and oppression in the design and use of educational assessments. 

I am engaged with Transcend to develop a measurement strategy for providing insight into the ways their partner schools are transforming to create equitable, 21st century learning environments.

My colleague Erika Landl and I are working with EdReports.org to develop and implement a set of tools to evaluate quality in interim assessment products. This contract is run through the Center for Assessment.

I am a partner to the Center for Collaborative Education, providing technical assistance for a consortium of public school districts who are creating a school quality reporting system that reflects the values of their communities. This contract is run through the Center for Assessment.

I teach a range of courses in Boston College's MESA department. I am currently teaching the Classroom Assessment course where I focus on culturally-sustaining classroom assessment practice. See the latest version of the course syllabus here, and selections of my students' writing here.

I lead a multi-year professional development effort for teams of science teachers in Guilford, CT. I engage them in a curriculum re-design process focused on aligning instruction and assessment to the richness and rigor of the Next Generation Science Standards. This contract is run through the Center for Assessment.

Since 2016 I have been working with the Massachusetts Association of 766 Approved Private Schools to conceptualize, design, and pilot a student outcomes and school quality reporting system that reflects the diverse set of schools serving students who need specialized, out-of-district services.
