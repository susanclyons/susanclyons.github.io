I am an independent consultant interested in the intersection of educational measurement and social justice. I work with clients to leverage the power of assessment to help move toward a more equitable future.

In addition to my consulting work, I am the Executive Director of a non-profit organization  aimed at advancing gender and racial equity in the field of educational measurement,  Women in Measurement, Inc.

Consulting Services

DESIGN 

I partner with clients to design innovative solutions related to educational assessment and accountability. I engage with my clients and their stakeholders to understand their goals, develop a strategy, and deliver creative solutions to complex problems. For example, since 2016 I have been working with the Massachusetts Association of 766 Approved Private Schools to conceptualize, design, and pilot a student outcomes and school quality reporting system that reflects the diverse set of schools serving students who need specialized, out-of-district services.

Teach

I develop and deliver sustained professional development opportunities for teachers, leaders, and assessment professionals. Topics include formative assessment, designing performance-based assessments, re-imagining school accountability, and moving toward culturally-sustaining item writing for large-scale assessment. I began my career as a seventh grade math teacher in Ecuador and I continue to enjoy teaching as a part-time faculty member at Boston College and through my consulting work.

Write

I collaborate with clients and colleagues to develop written products and presentations that serve to advance our thinking and share our work. My writing portfolio includes peer-reviewed academic research, technical reports, position papers, commentary on federal regulations, policy briefs, toolkits, and blog posts. My most recent writing is focused on addressing issues of racial inequity in the design and use of educational assessments. 

Advise

I serve as a technical adviser to assessment and accountability programs. I have particular interest and expertise in offering technical solutions under policy constraints in order to advance innovation in educational assessment. 

Selected Projects and Partners

Writing on racial equity in assessment

I am working closely with the Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion team at Cognia on a number of special projects aimed at advancing racial equity in the design and development of educational assessments. 

Developing measurement strategy

I am engaged with Transcend to develop a measurement strategy for providing insight into the ways their partner schools are transforming to create equitable, 21st century learning environments.   

Evaluating assessment quality

My colleague Erika Landl and I are working with EdReports.org to develop and implement a set of tools to evaluate quality in interim assessment products. This contract is run through the Center for Assessment. 

Advising on assessment system design

I am a partner to the Center for Collaborative Education, providing technical assistance for a consortium of public school districts who are creating a school quality reporting system that reflects the values of their communities. This contract is run through the Center for Assessment.

Teaching preservice educators

I teach a range of courses in Boston College's MESA department. I am currently teaching the Classroom Assessment course where I focus on culturally-sustaining classroom assessment practice. See the latest version of the course syllabus here, and selections of my students' writing here. 

Leading curricular
re-design

I lead a multi-year professional development effort for teams of science teachers in Guilford, CT. I engage them in a curriculum re-design process focused on aligning instruction and assessment to the richness and rigor of the Next Generation Science Standards. This contract is run through the Center for Assessment.
